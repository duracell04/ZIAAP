# ZIAAP Product Charter

**Version:** 1.2

**Current maturity:** C0 — interactive concept demonstrator

**Status:** Product/founder approval and legal-framing review pending

## Product promise

> **Test your dispute-resolution system before you sign the contract.**

ZIAAP helps parties align contractual meaning and test the observable behaviour
of an agreed dispute-resolution process before signing, using a governed AI
Resolution Officer to augment a properly appointed human arbitrator who retains
legal authority.

## Current artifact

The repository is a **high-fidelity interactive concept demonstrator**, where
high fidelity refers specifically to workflow and interaction fidelity. It uses
synthetic data, client-local resettable state, curated fixtures, and optional
live-unverified execution. It is not an externally validated system, legal
appointment, operative award process, production arbitration service, or
predictor of a future dispute result.

The official description is:

> ZIAAP is a high-fidelity interactive concept demonstrator of an AI-native
> dispute-governance protocol, with high fidelity at the workflow and
> interaction level. Using synthetic data and simulation-only state, it
> demonstrates how parties could align contractual meaning, test an agreed
> future resolution protocol and its observable behaviour, preserve procedural
> traceability, and support a human arbitrator. It is not an externally
> validated system, legal appointment, operative award process, or production
> arbitration service.

## Product thesis

Parties should make important dispute-governance choices while commercial
interests remain aligned. ZIAAP demonstrates a sequence in which parties state
expectations, expose divergence, structure agreed commercial terms, configure
the future protocol, observe that configuration under synthetic scenarios,
acknowledge the tested configuration, and preserve it for a later human-led
process.

The target capability is a **digital twin of the agreed dispute-resolution
process and its observable behaviour, not a predictor of the future result**.

## AI Resolution Officer boundary

The AI Resolution Officer is a governed software capability. It is not a legal
office, arbitral institution, arbitrator, or autonomous decision-maker. It may
structure, compare, retrieve, calculate, test, challenge, explain, and prepare
reviewable analysis. A properly appointed human arbitrator retains procedural
authority, independent judgment, and responsibility for any legally operative
decision.

## Six-stage lifecycle

1. **Party Alignment** — compare independent expectations and resolve divergence.
2. **Protocol Constitution** — configure rules, values, sources, tools,
   uncertainty, and escalation.
3. **Scenario Laboratory** — observe and interrogate the configured protocol.
4. **Configuration Manifest** — bind the tested synthetic configuration and its
   acknowledgements.
5. **Later Dispute** — demonstrate a bound, human-controlled later process.
6. **Audit Dossier** — project a truthful, inspectable lifecycle record.

## Hyper-minimal public concept experiment

The repository also contains a separate deterministic public walkthrough at
`/demo`. It is a comprehension experiment for business owners and
international-trade participants, not a new maturity level or a replacement for
the canonical lifecycle.

Its approved public hierarchy is:

> **Agree the rules before the dispute.**

> ZIAAP helps businesses uncover different contract expectations, test the
> agreed dispute rules, and prepare a source-linked case for a human arbitrator
> if conflict occurs.

> **AI prepares. Parties can challenge. A human arbitrator decides.**

The walkthrough projects the canonical lifecycle into five plain-language
steps:

| Public step | Canonical lifecycle projection |
|---|---|
| Align | Party Alignment |
| Test | Protocol Constitution, Scenario Laboratory and Configuration Manifest |
| Dispute | Later Dispute |
| Review | Human-controlled activity inside Later Dispute |
| Outcome | Audit Dossier |

This projection changes information architecture only. It does not create new
legal, technical or lifecycle authority.

The public walkthrough uses one synthetic SaaS clause, deterministic
client-local state, curated source excerpts, one party objection and one
simulated human modification. It makes no external model call and creates no
identity, appointment, signature, decision, award or legal effect.

Commercial-value statements in the walkthrough are hypotheses for unfamiliar
reader research. They are not evidence that the concept reduces cost, time,
disputes or professional workload.

## Evidence and authority boundaries

- Illustrative fixtures are curated simulations, not executions.
- Successful live calls are live-unverified, not independently validated.
- Deterministic tools perform formal calculations; language models do not
  validate their own outputs.
- Material propositions trace to recorded sources, rules, tools, uncertainty,
  challenges, or explicit unsupported status rather than private chain of
  thought.
- The concept creates no identity proof, institutional appointment, production
  signature, operative award, legal effect, or confidential production matter.

## Current release objective

The current objective is to accept and merge a technically clean,
terminologically disciplined, legally responsible, and externally
comprehensible concept baseline. The existing later-stage interface remains a
design reference, not completed roadmap capability.

The hyper-minimal public walkthrough is isolated from that reference and remains
an unaccepted concept-clarity experiment until its product/founder,
legal-framing and unfamiliar-reader evidence is recorded. The existing root
experience remains unchanged during that review.

Sprints 1–34 are provisional, funding-dependent future development. Prototype
planning, including any canonical domain model or Sprint 4 rebuild, begins only
after an angel-financing decision and a new prototype charter.
