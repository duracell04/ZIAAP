# ZIAAP Product Charter

**Version:** 1.6

**Current maturity:** C0 — interactive concept demonstrator

**Status:** Product direction approved 2026-07-24; legal-framing, privacy,
security, market, usability and production review pending

## Current product decision

> **ZIAAP is a scenario-based contract-alignment system for complex commercial
> agreements. It converts selected clauses into concrete operational scenarios,
> collects the parties' independent expectations, identifies materially
> different outcomes, and produces a versioned record of what was aligned,
> deliberately left open, or incorporated into the contract.**

The sole active wedge is scenario-based pre-signature alignment for three to
five high-impact clauses in complex enterprise systems-integration MSAs. The
[Pilot 01 Protocol](product/pilot-01-protocol.md) controls the product boundary,
state machine, legal-status schedule, neutrality charter and measurement
framework. It specifies research and runs none.

### Category thesis

> **Computational Private Ordering**

The category thesis is long term. The current sales and validation proposition
is ZIAAP Contract Alignment, not the entire lifecycle architecture.

### AI-convex product doctrine

> **ZIAAP's durable product is intended to be the governed production
> environment through which contractual ambiguity and divergence become
> structured, source-linked, versioned and contestable state—not a claim to
> superior AI legal answers.**

Models are intended to remain evaluated, version-bound proposal engines.
Parties control their submissions, confirmations and bilateral dispositions,
and properly authorised humans retain consequential decisions. A model cannot
reveal sealed responses, commit state, assign legal status or lock a version.

### Staged architecture and neutrality

Lifecycle re-alignment, purpose-segregated mediation and structured handover to
independent professionals or institutions are deferred extensions. The former
two-wedge execution strategy and partner-owned institution are superseded as
canonical near-term plans and preserved only as dated research history.

ZIAAP OpCo may own software and receive product revenue. It has no mediation,
appointment, evidence-assessment, adjudicative or award authority. Any later
neutral works through a separate engagement, independent appointment, isolated
access and economics that do not depend on platform use or outcome.

## Current artifact

The repository is a **high-fidelity interactive concept demonstrator**, where
high fidelity refers specifically to workflow and interaction fidelity. It uses
synthetic data, client-local resettable state, curated fixtures, and optional
live-unverified execution. It is not an externally validated system, legal
appointment, operative award process, production arbitration service, or
predictor of a future dispute result.

The official current-artifact description is:

> ZIAAP is a C0 high-fidelity interactive concept demonstrator, where high
> fidelity is limited to workflow and interaction. It uses synthetic,
> resettable, simulation-only state to illustrate contract alignment and a
> deferred human-controlled lifecycle. It is not an implemented Contract
> Alignment product, confidential matter system, legal service, pilot,
> appointment, award process or externally validated system.

## Product thesis

Parties should compare important operational expectations while commercial
interests remain aligned. ZIAAP Contract Alignment selects material clauses,
tests concrete scenarios, preserves original and normalised responses
separately, reveals confirmed material differences symmetrically and records
resolution, conscious openness or deferral without pretending that each state
has the same legal effect.

The initial target capability is a structured, versioned and contestable
alignment record. The structured dispute state and reasoning memorandum remain
later architecture, not the initial product proposition.

## Governed software boundary

Models may structure, compare, retrieve, calculate, explain and propose. They do
not control authoritative state, overwrite party-authored text, reveal sealed
responses, commit consequential transitions or create legal effect. The AI
Resolution Officer remains a governed software capability in the long-term
architecture, not a legal office, arbitral institution, arbitrator or
autonomous decision-maker.

## Three layers and six gates

| Layer | Gate | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| I0 · Flight Plan | 1. Alignment | Resolve material differences in operational contract expectations |
| I0 · Flight Plan | 2. Configuration | Assemble the Constitution, evidence policy, deterministic tools, scenario tests and permitted AI uses |
| I1 · Cockpit | 3. Appointment & Configuration Freeze | Identify the human arbitrator, freeze the configuration and record integrity evidence |
| I1 · Cockpit | 4. Case Production | Maintain the structured, versioned and contestable case state |
| I2 · Captain in Command | 5. Independent Adjudication | Preserve independent human review with zero algorithmic presumption |
| I2 · Captain in Command | 6. Award & Black Box | Produce any future human-signed award and preserve the procedural record |

The current demonstrator labels Gate 6 **Simulated Outcome & Procedural Black
Box**. It does not produce an award.

The Constitution is the versioned control plane that converts governance into
explicit system permissions, workflow boundaries, escalation rules and
invalidation conditions. “Machine-enforceable” refers only to software runtime
permissions and guards, not legal enforcement.

## Hyper-minimal public concept experiment

The repository also contains a deterministic public walkthrough at `/demo`.
It is a comprehension experiment, not a new maturity level, implemented product
or replacement for the canonical gates.

Its approved public hierarchy is:

> **Align expectations before you sign.**

> Turn selected clauses into operational scenarios, compare each party's
> independently confirmed expectations, and record what was aligned or
> deliberately left open.

> **AI structures scenarios. Each party confirms its own position.**

The walkthrough projects the six canonical gates into five plain-language
steps:

| Public step | Canonical gate projection |
|---|---|
| Align | Gate 1 · Alignment · current product |
| Test scenarios | Gate 2 · Configuration · current product |
| Structure dispute | Gate 4 · Case Production · future extension |
| Human review | Gate 5 · Independent Adjudication · future extension |
| Procedural record | Gate 6 · Simulated Outcome & Procedural Black Box · future extension |

This projection changes information architecture only. It does not create new
legal, technical or lifecycle authority.

The public walkthrough uses one synthetic SaaS clause, deterministic
client-local state, curated source excerpts, one party objection and one
simulated human modification. It makes no external model call and creates no
identity, appointment, signature, decision, award or legal effect.

Commercial-value statements in the walkthrough are hypotheses for unfamiliar
reader research. They are not evidence that the concept reduces cost, time,
disputes or professional workload.

The deployed root redirects to `/demo` for a bounded public pilot. The optional
`/feedback` form is the only public-path network exception: it posts low-risk
research responses through FormSubmit under the
[feedback-pilot governance](product/feedback-pilot-governance.md). Publication
does not satisfy the comprehension, legal-framing, privacy-governance or
product-approval gates.

## Evidence and authority boundaries

- Illustrative fixtures are curated simulations, not executions.
- Successful live calls are live-unverified, not independently validated.
- Deterministic tools perform formal calculations; language models do not
  validate their own outputs.
- Material propositions trace to recorded sources, rules, tools, uncertainty,
  challenges, or explicit unsupported status rather than private chain of
  thought.
- The concept creates no identity proof, institutional appointment, production
  signature, operative award, legal effect, or confidential production matter.

## Current release objective

The current objective is to review a technically clean, terminologically
disciplined C0 strategy reconciliation and complete Pilot 01 specification. The
expert interface continues to demonstrate the six gates as synthetic long-term
workflow reference; it does not implement Contract Alignment or later neutral
capability.

The hyper-minimal public walkthrough remains an unaccepted concept-clarity
experiment even though the deployed root now exposes it for a bounded pilot.
Product direction was approved on 2026-07-24. Legal-framing,
privacy-governance, security, market, usability, production and unfamiliar-
reader evidence remains pending.

The 2026-07-23 feedback plan remains a historical research record. Its two-
wedge comparison was superseded by the 2026-07-24 decision selecting Contract
Alignment. Neither record satisfies human acceptance, market validation or
authority to implement or run Pilot 01.

Sprints 1–34 remain provisional, funding-dependent future development. This
bounded refactor authorises no backend, identity, production data, real
appointment, corporate implementation, signature or award work.
