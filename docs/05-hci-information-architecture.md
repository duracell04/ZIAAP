# HCI and Information Architecture

## Self-Explaining Synthetic Showcase

## 1. Experience objective

The interface should answer, in order: what problem exists, why governance begins
before conflict, what parties configure, what AI contributes, what a human
controls, what was illustrated or executed, and what the prototype cannot prove.

## 2. Opening information architecture

The opening contains:

1. canonical definition and commercial problem;
2. pre-conflict value proposition;
3. human-arbitrator versus ZIAAP-protocol comparison;
4. seven-step lifecycle;
5. guided and explore entry actions; and
6. persistent synthetic, simulation-only disclosure.

Guided mode begins at Party Alignment. Explore mode permits direct navigation
while retaining truthful incomplete-state indicators. Both provide return to the
opening.

## 3. Workspace navigation

1. Party Alignment;
2. Arbitral Reasoning Calibration;
3. Stress Testing;
4. Protocol Manifest and Simulated Appointment; and
5. Later Synthetic Dispute.

Navigation labels describe simulation, not legal completion. The global header
shows lifecycle mode, exact-manifest readiness, and lifecycle status.

## 4. Party Alignment composition

The screen uses progressive sections:

- source contract;
- two independent party profiles;
- separate illustrative/live analysis controls;
- authority and limitations strip;
- material-type legend;
- divergence cards with positions, consequence, sources, and uncertainty;
- selectable option and detailed consequence panel;
- legal constraint treatment;
- editable revised text;
- deterministic scenario card;
- exact-version confirmations; and
- locked or generated Alignment Annex.

Edits expose stale or invalidated state rather than silently carrying prior
readiness forward.

## 5. Semantic grammar

| Material | Treatment | Required label |
|---|---|---|
| Source text | neutral paper | `SOURCE TEXT` |
| Party assertion | amber/party context | `PARTY ASSERTION` |
| AI-supported analysis | reasoning blue | `AI-SUPPORTED ANALYSIS` plus execution status |
| Legal constraint | red rule | `LEGAL CONSTRAINT · REVIEW REQUIRED` |
| Deterministic calculation | green result | formula, inputs, and blocked/calculated state |
| Proposed option | neutral selection | `PROPOSED OPTION` |
| Agreed text | green confirmation | exact version and both parties |

Authority metadata is never encoded by color alone.

## 6. Execution states

- illustrative selected;
- live loading;
- executed-unverified selected;
- live failed with preserved inactive artifact;
- stale after input edits; and
- deliberate recovery through illustrative selection or successful rerun.

Failures use `role=status` or equivalent announcement and state why acknowledgement
is blocked.

## 7. Manifest interaction

The UI readiness predicate may enable a button but is not an integrity proof. The
async transition exposes pending, accepted, and rejected states. The digest is
called **Exact protocol manifest** and its exclusions are visible near the hash.

## 8. Accessibility and responsive behavior

All controls are keyboard operable, focus-visible, and labelled. Headings follow
document order. Status does not rely on color. At narrow widths, two-column roles,
profiles, positions, options, metadata, and lifecycle elements stack. Horizontal
scroll is limited to intentional local navigation, never the page.

## 9. Print

Print hides navigation and controls, preserves authority metadata, and prints the
Alignment Annex as a presentation artifact with its synthetic/no-effect boundary.

## 10. Reviewer comprehension targets

The reviewer should explain the problem, pre-conflict timing, Party Alignment,
AI contribution, human control, illustrative versus live execution, genuine
validation boundary, manifest meaning, and most valuable output without coaching.
