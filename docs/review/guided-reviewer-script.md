# ZIAAP Alpha Guided Reviewer Script

## Purpose

Test whether the opening and Party Alignment alpha explain ZIAAP without presenter
interpretation. Do not teach the reviewer the answers. Record confusion verbatim.

## Reviewer profile

Record role, relevant arbitration/commercial/technology experience, and prior
knowledge of ZIAAP. Do not collect confidential client or dispute information.

## Session setup

- Use the public illustrative showcase or the local procedure.
- Reset the demo before each session.
- Ask the reviewer to think aloud.
- Do not enable live execution; the review target is deterministic.
- Target 20–30 minutes.

## Part 1 — Unassisted opening (5 minutes)

Show only the opening screen. Say:

> Please explore this page as if nobody were available to explain it. Tell me
> what you think the product is, what problem it addresses, and anything that
> feels unclear or overstated.

Do not prompt for specific wording. Record:

- first interpretation;
- human/protocol distinction;
- meaning of simulation-only disclosure;
- which entry action they choose; and
- time to a coherent explanation.

## Part 2 — Party Alignment task (10–15 minutes)

Ask the reviewer to:

1. begin the guided demonstration;
2. inspect the draft and independent party expectations;
3. explain one divergence in their own words;
4. identify source text, party assertion, AI-supported analysis, legal constraint,
   deterministic calculation, proposed option, and agreed text;
5. compare the uptime options;
6. explain the CHF 1,500 deterministic result;
7. confirm both profiles;
8. select **View illustrative example**;
9. confirm all exact clause versions; and
10. explain how the Alignment Annex was produced.

Observe without rescuing: navigation, status comprehension, source use, option
trade-offs, legal-review boundary, version confirmation, and Annex provenance.

## Part 3 — Failure comprehension (3 minutes)

Reset if needed, then ask the reviewer to select **Run live execution** in the
public/local default environment. Ask:

- What happened?
- Is the prior illustrative analysis still usable?
- What must you do before simulated acknowledgement can continue?

Expected behavior: explicit failure reason, preserved findings, inactive artifact,
and deliberate illustrative recovery. Do not explain this before they answer.

## Part 4 — Structured questions (5 minutes)

Use the questionnaire verbatim. Ask for confidence from 1 (guessing) to 5
(certain) after each answer. Do not mark an answer correct during the session.

## Close

Ask:

- What was most commercially valuable?
- What felt like theatre rather than product value?
- Which phrase or screen created the greatest risk of misunderstanding?
- What would you remove?
- What would you need to see next?

Thank the reviewer and save notes in a new copy of the findings log. Do not merge
findings across reviewers until at least five representative sessions are complete.
