# ZIAAP Review-Candidate Guided Reviewer Script

## Purpose

Test whether the complete review candidate explains ZIAAP without presenter
interpretation. Do not teach the reviewer the answers. Record confusion verbatim.
No session result should be generalized into validation without the planned next-
cycle analysis.

## Reviewer profile and setup

Record role, relevant arbitration/commercial/technology experience, device, and
prior knowledge of ZIAAP. Do not collect confidential client or dispute data.

- Use `prototype-showcase-v1.0-review-candidate`.
- Reset before every session.
- Ask the reviewer to think aloud.
- Keep public live execution disabled; test its failure state separately.
- Target 35–50 minutes.

## Part 1 — Unassisted opening

Show only the opening. Say:

> Please explore this page as if nobody were available to explain it. Tell me
> what you think the product is, what problem it addresses, and anything that
> feels unclear or overstated.

Record the first interpretation, pre-conflict rationale, human/protocol
distinction, simulation boundary, chosen entry action, and time to a coherent
explanation.

## Part 2 — Party Alignment

Ask the reviewer to:

1. begin the guided demonstration;
2. inspect the draft and independent expectations;
3. explain one divergence and its source/authority boundary;
4. distinguish all seven material types;
5. compare the uptime options and explain the CHF 1,500 deterministic result;
6. confirm both profiles and select **View illustrative example**;
7. confirm all exact clause versions; and
8. explain how the Alignment Annex emerged.

## Part 3 — Arbitral Reasoning Calibration

Ask the reviewer to explain:

- what is being configured;
- why model weights remain unchanged;
- how interpretation, evidence, fairness, commercial values, remedies,
  discretion, uncertainty, escalation, model/tool policy, and source hierarchy
  differ;
- what the settlement firewall and change policy do; and
- what exact Constitution acknowledgement means and does not mean.

Have both simulated parties acknowledge the displayed Constitution version.

## Part 4 — Stress Testing

Select **View illustrative example**. For one scenario, ask the reviewer to point
to hypothetical facts, evidence, required safeguards, observed behavior,
execution status, limitations, provenance, and consequence. Ask how stress testing
differs from calibration and genuine independent validation.

Have both parties acknowledge all four exact artifacts for the simulated ceremony.

## Part 5 — Exact Protocol Manifest and Simulated Appointment

Ask the reviewer to inspect the full manifest and explain what it identifies and
what the digest cannot prove. Then:

1. prepare the manifest;
2. acknowledge the exact hash as both parties;
3. review the fictional disclosure;
4. record fictional arbitrator acceptance; and
5. simulate appointment under the acknowledged manifest.

Ask whether any interaction created a legal appointment, signature, identity
proof, provider proof, runtime attestation, or validation.

## Part 6 — Later Synthetic Dispute

Ask the reviewer to trace the numbered sequence:

1. acknowledged protocol identified;
2. shared merits record assembled;
3. optional sealed settlement track;
4. fictional human pre-assessment;
5. provisional simulation-only determination; and
6. fictional human decision.

Activate settlement, generate the proposal, and decline it as one party. Confirm
that proposal terms remain outside the merits-facing determination. Record a
fictional preliminary assessment, select the illustrative determination, then
adopt/modify/reject it with an independent rationale and checklist.

## Part 7 — Demonstration Dossier

Open the dossier. Ask the reviewer to:

- confirm all twelve artifacts are available;
- choose the three most commercially valuable artifacts;
- locate actor, authority, lifecycle mode, execution status, version, provenance,
  consequence, and legal-effect boundary;
- explain why external validation is still pending; and
- assess whether the print layout would support an expert discussion.

## Part 8 — Failure and reset comprehension

After reset, select **Run live execution** in the default public environment. Ask:

- What happened?
- Was the preserved fixture silently substituted as success?
- Why is acknowledgement blocked?
- What deliberate action recovers the illustrative path?

Then reset again and confirm the full initial synthetic state is restored.

## Part 9 — Structured questionnaire and close

Use the questionnaire verbatim and record confidence from 1 (guessing) to 5
(certain). Do not mark answers correct during the session.

Close with:

- What was most commercially valuable?
- What felt like theatre rather than product value?
- Which phrase or screen created the greatest misunderstanding risk?
- What should be removed?
- What evidence would be required for genuine validation?

Save notes in a new findings-log copy. Do not infer aggregate findings until the
next-cycle evaluation protocol is executed.
