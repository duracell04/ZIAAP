# Sprint 0 Human Acceptance Packet

**Status:** Prepared; no reviewer participation or outcome is inferred

## Purpose

This packet tests whether the C0 concept demonstrator is understandable,
credible, commercially meaningful, and explicit about its authority and
technical boundaries without presenter coaching.

Human review begins only after the technical acceptance diff has been reviewed
and the exact candidate commit has been recorded. Use synthetic data only.

## Required independent reviewers

1. One arbitrator, arbitration lawyer, or dispute-resolution professional with
   no prior involvement in ZIAAP.
2. One business owner, commercial decision-maker, angel investor, or technology
   investor with no prior involvement in ZIAAP.

## Central comprehension proposition

Each reviewer should independently reach this idea:

> ZIAAP lets parties inspect and test the agreed dispute-resolution process and
> its observable behaviour before signing, while preserving human arbitral
> authority.

Without coaching, each reviewer must accurately explain:

1. what ZIAAP is;
2. what the current demonstrator actually demonstrates;
3. what it does not yet implement or prove;
4. what the AI Resolution Officer does;
5. why the human arbitrator retains authority;
6. what testing the future resolution protocol means;
7. why this is valuable before signing an international commercial contract;
   and
8. why the result is a concept demonstrator rather than a prototype.

## Packet contents and use order

1. Send the [`review invitation`](review-invitation.md).
2. Give the reviewer the [`expert feedback instructions`](expert-feedback-instructions.md).
3. Prepare the exact candidate using the
   [`local demonstration procedure`](local-demonstration-procedure.md).
4. Conduct the session with the
   [`guided reviewer script`](guided-reviewer-script.md), preserving its
   unassisted opening and no-coaching rule.
5. Ask the [`reviewer questionnaire`](reviewer-questionnaire.md) verbatim.
6. Save one de-identified copy of the
   [`findings log template`](findings-log-template.md) for that reviewer.
7. Reference both completed findings logs from the
   [`Sprint 0 acceptance record`](sprint-0-acceptance-record.md).

## Acceptance rule

- Both required reviewer profiles must complete independent sessions.
- Each reviewer must explain all eight comprehension points accurately.
- Each reviewer must produce zero authority-category errors.
- Every misunderstanding is recorded as a finding rather than corrected during
  the task.
- An authority-category error blocks acceptance until the relevant language or
  experience is corrected and independently retested.
- Blank templates, invitations, protocols, and prepared forms are not completed
  evidence.

## Product and legal-framing reviews

The product/founder decision and the legal-framing and authority-boundary review
use the blank records in the Sprint 0 acceptance record. They require actual
identity, capacity, candidate commit, decision, date/timezone, and evidence
reference.

The legal-framing review is limited by this statement:

> This review concerns the accuracy of the concept's legal framing and
> disclaimers. It is not an opinion on enforceability, regulatory compliance, or
> production readiness.

## Technical and product boundary

Sprint 0 does not require or demonstrate a local LLM, persistent matters,
authenticated users, separate party sessions, confidential-document handling,
production model execution, digital signatures, institutional appointment,
external validation, or an operative award process. Human reviewers must not be
shown machine verification as proof of any of those capabilities.
