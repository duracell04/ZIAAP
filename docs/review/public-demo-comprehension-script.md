# ZIAAP Hyper-Minimal Public Concept Comprehension Script

**Status:** Protocol only; no participant evidence recorded

## Purpose

Test whether an unfamiliar business or international-trade participant can
understand the four-minute `/demo` walkthrough without presenter explanation.
This is formative concept research, not independent validation.

Do not teach answers, correct the participant during the walkthrough, or
describe confusion as user failure. Record the participant’s exact words.

## Required cohorts

- At least five unfamiliar business-owner, commercial or international-trade
  participants.
- At least three unfamiliar arbitration or dispute-resolution professionals.
- No participant may have helped design, build or review ZIAAP.

Record reviewer ID, role, relevant experience, device, viewport, prior ZIAAP
knowledge, build commit, date and timezone. Do not collect confidential matter
data.

## Session procedure

1. Reset `/demo` and show only its opening screen.
2. Ask: “Please use this walkthrough without my help. Tell me what you think is
   happening and anything that is unclear.”
3. Start a timer when the participant selects **Start the 4-minute simulation**.
4. Do not prompt the participant toward **Compare expectations**, **Show
   source**, **Contest this inference**, **Modify** or any later action.
5. Stop the timer when the Outcome screen is understood or the participant
   cannot continue.
6. Ask the public-walkthrough addendum in
   [`reviewer-questionnaire.md`](reviewer-questionnaire.md) verbatim.
7. Save a de-identified findings-log copy. Leave every result pending until the
   evidence is reviewed.

## Observations to record

- Time to a coherent unaided explanation.
- Total walkthrough time.
- Whether the participant distinguishes the original clause, expectations and
  revised simulated wording.
- Whether the participant understands why the missing log period requires human
  review.
- Whether the participant finds a **Show source** action.
- Whether the participant finds and understands **Contest this inference**.
- Whether the participant notices that the human assessment precedes AI
  disclosure.
- Whether the participant explains the change from 15% / CHF 1,500 to 10% /
  CHF 1,000.
- Any belief that AI, ZIAAP or a fictional record holds legal authority.
- Any perceived business value or adoption barrier.
- Whether “commercial aviation” is understood as governed systems around
  professional judgment rather than mechanical outcome predictability.

## Promotion thresholds

The public walkthrough may replace the root opening only when:

- median walkthrough time is four minutes or less;
- at least four of five business participants explain before-conflict alignment
  unaided;
- every participant identifies the human arbitrator as the decision-maker;
- at least four of five business participants identify one concrete potential
  commercial benefit;
- at least four of five business participants find the source and understand
  party challenge;
- no participant believes the demonstration appoints an arbitrator, issues an
  enforceable award, executes live AI or provides validated legal conclusions;
- no participant interprets the aviation analogy as mechanical or predictable
  legal outcomes;
- every visible control works;
- product/founder approval and legal-framing review are recorded; and
- corporate-governance review confirms that no current partner-ownership fact is
  implied.

Any authority-category error blocks promotion. Any other threshold miss
requires revision and retesting of the affected cohort. Blank evidence fields
remain pending and cannot be inferred from this protocol.
