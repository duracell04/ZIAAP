# ZIAAP Public Concept Comprehension Script

**Status:** Protocol only; no participant evidence recorded

The 2026-07-24 product-direction decision does not authorise recruitment or
execution of this protocol.

## Purpose

Test whether an unfamiliar business or systems-integration participant can
understand the four-minute `/demo` walkthrough without presenter explanation.
This is formative concept research, not independent validation or Pilot 01.

Do not teach answers, correct the participant during the walkthrough or
describe confusion as user failure. Under an approved future charter, record
the participant's exact words and use no confidential contract or matter data.

## Required future cohorts

- At least five unfamiliar systems-integration business or commercial
  participants.
- At least three unfamiliar commercial-contract or dispute-resolution
  professionals.
- No participant may have helped design, build or review ZIAAP.

Record reviewer ID, role, relevant experience, device, viewport, prior ZIAAP
knowledge, build commit, date and timezone.

## Session procedure

1. Reset `/demo` and show only its opening screen.
2. Ask: "Please use this walkthrough without my help. Tell me what you think is
   happening and anything that is unclear."
3. Start a timer when the participant selects **Start the 4-minute simulation**.
4. Do not prompt the participant toward any action.
5. Stop the timer when the final screen is understood or the participant cannot
   continue.
6. Ask the public-walkthrough addendum in
   [`reviewer-questionnaire.md`](reviewer-questionnaire.md) verbatim.
7. Under an approved research charter, save a de-identified findings log. Leave
   every result pending until the evidence is reviewed.

## Observations to record

- Time to a coherent unaided explanation and total walkthrough time.
- Whether the participant identifies systems-integration contract alignment as
  the current wedge.
- Whether the participant distinguishes Align and Test scenarios as current
  from Structure dispute, Human review and Procedural record as future.
- Whether the participant distinguishes original party text from AI-normalised
  or inferred text.
- Whether the participant understands that a model cannot confirm a party's
  position, reveal responses, assign legal status or lock a version.
- Whether the participant finds source, correction and contest actions.
- Whether the participant understands the human-authority boundary in the
  future dispute illustration.
- Any belief that AI, ZIAAP or a fictional record holds legal authority.
- Any perceived business value or adoption barrier.

## Comprehension acceptance thresholds

Operational publication is not evidence that the public walkthrough passed
this protocol. Any future positive comprehension claim requires:

- median walkthrough time is four minutes or less;
- at least four of five business participants explain pre-signature alignment
  unaided;
- every participant distinguishes the current wedge from future extensions;
- every participant identifies the parties as controllers of current alignment
  positions and adoption;
- at least four of five business participants identify one concrete potential
  commercial benefit;
- at least four of five business participants find the source and understand
  party correction or challenge;
- no participant believes the demonstration accepts a real contract, appoints
  a neutral, issues an enforceable result, executes live AI, creates legal
  effect or provides validated legal conclusions;
- every visible control works;
- product approval and legal-framing review are recorded; and
- privacy and security review confirms the authorised research boundary.

Any authority-category error blocks a positive acceptance claim. Any other
threshold miss requires revision and retesting of the affected cohort. Blank
evidence fields remain pending and cannot be inferred from publication,
submissions or this protocol.
