## The strategic question to resolve

The current operating model starts with Zero Instance/I0 and carries a future
hypothesis that a partner-owned institution could ultimately operate the full
model. The memorandum proposes starting with tribunal case intelligence
delivered through existing institutions and adding I0 later. These are
different first products, buyers, adoption paths and capital plans.

The research programme must compare them without assuming that either is the
answer.

### AI-convex doctrine across both wedges

The following doctrine applies equally to both commercial hypotheses. It does
not select a wedge, prove demand or portability, reorder the roadmap, or
authorise technical implementation:

> **ZIAAP's durable product is not a claim to superior AI legal answers. It is
> the governed production environment through which contractual ambiguity and
> divergence become structured, source-linked, versioned and contestable
> state. Models are evaluated, version-bound proposal engines; they never
> control the authoritative record, execute consequential transitions or
> exercise legal authority. Parties retain challenge rights, and properly
> authorised humans retain consequential decisions.**

Answer quality, citation fidelity and legal-quality non-inferiority remain
mandatory guardrails, not the claimed moat. The process may produce
clarification, deliberately preserved ambiguity, incomplete agreement or
unresolved divergence; it must not relabel every ambiguity as resolved.

Responsibility remains allocated as follows:

- ZIAAP structures state, evidence, permissions and transition controls;
- parties control their submissions and objections;
- applicable law and validly adopted procedure govern procedural authority;
  and
- properly authorised humans control consequential decisions.

This is a process-governance hypothesis to falsify through both wedge studies.
The present C0 demonstrator remains provider-specific and does not establish
provider flexibility, local execution, sovereign deployment or behaviour
equivalence between models.

| Field | Wedge A: embedded bilateral I0 diagnostic | Wedge B: institution/tribunal case-intelligence workspace |
|---|---|---|
| Product hypothesis | Detect and govern interpretation gaps before signature, renewal or conflict | Structure a closed or synthetic dispute record for tribunal review without transferring legal authority |
| Initial buyer hypothesis to test | One commercial-contracting or legal-operations owner with budget and workflow authority | One institutional case-management or product owner; research must determine whether an institution or tribunal participant actually controls payment |
| Painful workflow hypothesis | Repeated manual comparison of draft language, operational expectations and unresolved commercial exposure | Repeated manual reconstruction of issues, evidence, chronology, objections and open questions |
| Adoption authority to establish | Contract owner, legal/procurement process owner, security/privacy reviewer and any CLM administrator | Institution, parties and tribunal roles required by the applicable agreement, rules and procedural order, plus security/privacy approval |
| Payment trigger to test | A defined contracting event where reduced review effort or better attention allocation justifies spend | A defined administered matter or case-production event where measurable professional time or quality improvement justifies spend |
| Delivery hypothesis | Word, CLM or API integration; not a dedicated dispute workspace | Dedicated neutral workspace; not a redefinition of I1 as negotiation or mediation |
| Main implementation burden to measure | Bilateral participation, document integration, versioning, data separation and amendment workflow | Neutrality, procedural adoption, confidentiality, evidence provenance, objections, model governance and institution integration |
| Measurable advantage required | Better issue coverage, traceability or review effort than both manual work and a general model | Better case-state coverage, traceability or review effort than both manual work and a general model |

The eventual scorecard must identify **one buyer, one painful workflow, one
adoption authority path, one payment trigger, one baseline cost, one
implementation burden and one measurable advantage** for each wedge. A broad
list of possible users or benefits does not satisfy the gate.

## Candidate agreement and ambiguity states

The following is a **validation taxonomy**, not an implemented schema or an
addition to the canonical glossary. Research must determine whether users can
distinguish the states, whether they change behaviour, and which state changes
may permit Gate 1 to proceed.

| Candidate state | Required meaning | Prohibited inference |
|---|---|---|
| Individual expectation | A versioned assertion attributable to one party | Shared agreement or contractual text |
| Unresolved divergence | Compared positions remain materially different | The system resolved the difference |
| Clarified text | Parties selected and exactly confirmed a proposed wording version in the simulated workflow | Enforceability or external incorporation |
| Deliberately preserved ambiguity | The parties knowingly record a specific ambiguity and why it remains open | Accidental omission, hidden agreement or automatic Gate 1 readiness |
| Incomplete agreement | An essential term has not been agreed | A contract-complete state |
| Non-binding scenario response | A party response to a hypothetical outcome or configuration test | ZIAAP treating or routing it as an amendment, consent or merits admission; external treatment remains subject to applicable law and adopted procedure |
| Formally incorporated amendment | A record that the required evidence of a legally effective external act was accepted under the applicable process | A software label or ZIAAP version creating contractual or legal effect |

The research hypothesis is that an incomplete essential term blocks Gate 1. A
non-essential deliberately preserved ambiguity may proceed only if the exact
open point, treatment rule, escalation path and bilateral acknowledgement are
recorded. That rule is not adopted until legal, product and user testing
supports it.

Any future incorporation flow must create a new version and digest, invalidate
stale confirmations and downstream artifacts, and distinguish the external
legal act from ZIAAP's record of it. In C0, every such example remains synthetic
and without legal effect.

## Candidate commercial-attention triage

The proposed triage is an explainable negotiation-attention aid. It must not
rank legal validity, predict adjudication or hide professional judgment behind
a number.

Every future triage artifact would need:

- factor inputs such as plausible economic exposure range, frequency,
  operational dependency, reversibility, evidence availability and the cost of
  resolving the point;
- the source, provenance and uncertainty for each input;
- an attention band and plain-language rationale;
- an explicit `illustrative heuristic / no legal effect` label;
- missing-input warnings; and
- an attributable human override that preserves the original output.

Candidate attention actions include resolve before signing, price or allocate,
record a deliberate ambiguity with controls, and defer below an agreed
threshold. The research must test these labels before they become product
terminology. A composite numeric score is out of scope unless its calibration,
uncertainty and user interpretation can be supported empirically.

## Source and evidence hierarchy

The force of a source depends on its role in the analysed deployment, not the
prestige of its publisher.

| Level | Category | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Binding law and controlling decisions | Apply within their jurisdiction and scope; identify the connecting factor and date |
| 2 | Adopted procedure | Arbitration agreement, incorporated rules, party agreement and procedural orders, subject to mandatory law |
| 3 | Soft law and professional guidance | Non-binding as guidance; identified terms may acquire contractual or procedural force only to the extent validly adopted |
| 4 | Empirical and scholarly evidence | Supports a bounded empirical proposition, with method and limitations |
| 5 | Institutional or vendor statements | Establishes only what the publisher publicly describes, unless independently verified |
| 6 | Strategic inference and product hypothesis | A proposition to test, not evidence of legality, performance, adoption or demand |

Institutional rules are not binding law. They may become part of the adopted
procedure. Vendor and institutional publications do not validate ZIAAP.

## Corrected legal and governance research snapshot

This section is research analysis as at 2026-07-23, not a Swiss legal opinion.
It must be rechecked before external publication or any consequential use.

### Comparative AI-arbitration authority

Research through the record date did not identify a reported Swiss decision
setting aside an award merely because AI was used. The closest identified
AI-specific comparative authority is
[*ARIHQ c. Santé Québec*, 2026 QCCS 1360](https://www.justice-quebec.ca/_downloads/961f025dc1ef82e4836d849d3c3cded8)
(Québec Superior Court, 22 April 2026), especially paragraphs 102–121.

Applying Québec domestic arbitration law, the court annulled an award where
the authorities central to its legal reasoning were nonexistent and the
evidence supported delegation of the decisional task and failure to review.
The court expressly rejected an automatic rule: limited AI use, an isolated
error, or use on a less important point could produce a different result.
Procedural integrity and material impact were decisive. The decision is
comparative, non-Swiss authority; it does not make AI use a freestanding Swiss
annulment ground.

The specific
[*Mata v. Avianca* sanctions order](https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/district-courts/new-york/nysdce/1%3A2022cv01461/575368/54/)
remains relevant only as a comparison concerning counsel's verification of
AI-assisted work. It is not arbitral-validity authority.

### Swiss arbitration pathways and foreign enforcement

| Context | Primary framework | Required distinction |
|---|---|---|
| Swiss-seated international arbitration | [PILA Chapter 12](https://www.fedlex.admin.ch/eli/cc/1988/1776_1776_1776/en), including Articles 176, 182 and 190–191 | Determine scope, agreed procedure, equal treatment/right to be heard and whether a pleaded defect engages an exhaustive statutory ground |
| Swiss-seated domestic arbitration | [Swiss CPC Part 3](https://www.fedlex.admin.ch/eli/cc/2010/262/en), including the recourse regime in Articles 389–393 | Do not collapse domestic recourse into PILA Chapter 12 |
| Recognition or enforcement of a foreign or non-domestic award | [New York Convention](https://uncitral.un.org/en/texts/arbitration/conventions/foreign_arbitral_awards), particularly Article V | Distinguish refusal of recognition/enforcement from setting aside at the seat |

An alleged AI-related defect must connect to an applicable ground; it is not a
ground by itself. Convention-state participation creates a framework, not a
guarantee that a particular award or process will be recognised or enforced.

### EU AI Act

Classification is feature- and intended-use-specific. Annex III 8(a) of the
[EU AI Act](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj) includes systems
used in a similar way in alternative dispute resolution to assist in
researching and interpreting facts or law and applying law to concrete facts.
The Article 6 analysis, including any documented Article 6(3) pathway, must be
performed for the actual function. A tribunal-facing merits or disposition
feature may therefore differ from genuinely ancillary administration,
chronology or routing. An I0 contract diagnostic is not within Annex III 8(a)
merely because it processes legal text.

Human review alone neither determines classification nor completes compliance.
Territorial scope, actor role, degree of influence, deployer controls under
Article 26 and any Article 50 transparency duty must be assessed only where
their triggers are met. Classification and scope are distinct from when a
specific obligation applies. Before relying on this analysis, check Article
113, the applicable transitional provisions and any enacted amendment in force
on the deployment or publication date; this snapshot does not state that
Articles 26 or 50 were already applicable on the record date.

### Swiss data protection, confidentiality and privilege

Human-in-the-loop is not a complete analysis under the
[Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection](https://www.fedlex.admin.ch/eli/cc/2022/491/en).
For each feature, future work must document:

- who determines purposes and means and the controller, processor and
  subprocessor roles;
- data categories and sources, purpose, access, retention, deletion and any
  provider-training use;
- processing and transfer locations, safeguards and security controls;
- transparency obligations and whether Article 21 on exclusively automated
  decisions with legal or similarly significant effects is engaged; and
- whether the proposed processing creates high risk requiring a
  [data-protection impact assessment](https://www.edoeb.admin.ch/en/data-protection-impact-assessment).

The analysis must cover private expectation profiles, case ingestion,
evidence/materiality triage, tribunal intelligence and cross-border provider
processing. Privilege, professional secrecy and arbitral confidentiality are
separate questions. The
[FDPIC AI guidance](https://www.edoeb.admin.ch/en/ai-and-data-protection),
[information-duty guidance](https://www.edoeb.admin.ch/en/duty-to-provide-information)
and [outsourcing guidance](https://www.edoeb.admin.ch/en/outsourcing-of-data-processing)
are regulator guidance, not approval of ZIAAP.

### CIArb guidance and provider flexibility

The
[CIArb Guideline on the Use of AI in Arbitration](https://www.ciarb.org/media/bpndtcgu/guideline-on-the-use-of-ai-in-arbitration_updated-sept-2025.pdf)
is non-binding professional guidance. Specified provisions may form part of the
adopted procedure to the extent incorporated by party agreement or procedural
order, subject to mandatory law and tribunal powers. It provides both
pre-dispute and case-specific governance patterns, permits specified permitted
or prohibited tools or characteristics, and includes a procedural-order
template. It does not legally require a model-neutral clause.

ZIAAP should distinguish stable pre-dispute governance principles from the
provider, model and version selected for a particular matter before the
configuration freeze. Provider-flexible does not mean behaviour-independent.
Every behaviour-affecting replacement requires task-specific evaluation,
provenance, re-testing, refreshed approval, invalidation of stale approvals or
outputs where relevant, monitored rollout and rollback. No silent substitution
is permitted.

## Ninety-day research-only programme

Day numbers begin only when a named research owner starts the programme. All
work is limited to interviews, concierge workflows, closed or synthetic
matters and tabletop pilots. It does not authorise a live consequential
arbitration pilot.

### Days 1–10: freeze the research protocol

- Name one research owner and one decision owner.
- Define one segment and one candidate buyer role per wedge.
- Freeze interview questions, pricing tests, benchmark instructions, scoring
  rubric, consent language and data-rights checks before collecting evidence.
- Define every metric, unit, denominator, aggregation rule, uncertainty display,
  non-inferiority guardrail and hard-stop invariant. Predeclare how missing,
  tied and failed runs will be treated and the one bounded repeat rule for
  ordinary functional or execution failures; governance incidents are excluded.
- Predeclare how buyer discovery will set a minimum material improvement for
  each wedge. Freeze the numerical thresholds by Day 35, before any comparative
  output is seen; they cannot be revised after benchmark execution begins.
- Predeclare the method and variables used to derive cross-wedge equivalence and
  materially-better tolerances, plus the threshold-setting/confirmation sample
  split. The numerical values are frozen from the threshold-setting sample, not
  in Days 1–10. Because the units differ, do not create an unvalidated composite
  score.
- Choose an executed parallel or randomised, counterbalanced crossover design.
  Record expertise matching, matter assignment, time/tool/support parity,
  evaluator blinding, order-effect controls and the number of independent
  stochastic runs.
- Select at least eight eligible closed or synthetic matters spanning the
  challenge cases below. Reserve at least two as unseen confirmatory holdouts
  under an independent custodian; record why each matter is representative.
- Freeze holdout allocation, custody, access embargo and unblinding rules. A
  product/research operator may not inspect holdout facts before the relevant
  design version is frozen. A previously inspected matter is not a holdout, and
  any post-hoc Narrow or changed design requires evidence from a new independent
  organisation and matter; obtain a replacement if the reserve is exhausted.
- Require the independent custodian to pre-tag holdout challenge coverage
  without revealing matter facts. A corrective holdout must exercise the
  specific failed invariant; if the reserve lacks two such matters, acquire
  eligible independent replacements before the corrected version can Pass.
- Define a blinded expert-reference and disagreement-resolution method for
  issue and evidence scoring.
- Freeze a date-stamped rule for selecting the frontier general-model
  comparator before seeing any output. Record the provider, model, version,
  date, prompts, tools and source pack for every model-assisted condition.
- Give the comparator and ZIAAP condition the same source/tool access and the
  same predeclared prompt-tuning and human-verification budget.
- Predeclare both the whole-product comparison and a same-model ablation. In
  the ablation, hold the model snapshot, source pack, tool access and human
  budget constant while comparing the general prompt-and-document workflow
  with the structured ZIAAP workflow. If the ordinary ZIAAP condition uses a
  different model, add the matched structured condition rather than inferring
  process advantage from the whole-product comparison.
- Predeclare whether the study will repeat that ablation with at least two
  model families. Until two families complete the same frozen protocol, no
  result may support a claim that the process advantage survives model changes
  or that ZIAAP is provider-flexible in operation. Cross-family testing assesses
  robustness of the process hypothesis, not equivalence between models.

### Research-data no-go control

“Closed” does not mean available for AI processing. Before any team member,
operator or provider accesses a non-synthetic matter, the rights and consent
register must record a **Pass** for that matter and each proposed condition:

- the data supplier's authority to provide and permit the intended research use;
- lawful basis or consent and any limits on secondary use;
- confidentiality, privilege and professional-secrecy treatment;
- de-identification method and re-identification risk;
- provider training, retention, transfer and subprocessor settings;
- storage, access, logging, deletion date and deletion owner; and
- a secure research environment plus a documented incident/notification plan,
  named incident owner and tested containment, access-revocation and deletion
  procedure; and
- the named accountable approver and supporting evidence.

Any unresolved item forces use of a synthetic matter. Approval for one
condition does not transfer to another provider, model, operator or purpose.
Protected closed data remains prohibited until the response controls exist and
pass the condition-specific review.

### Days 11–35: buyer, workflow and adoption discovery

For each wedge, conduct at least 12 de-identified interviews across at least
eight independent organisations, with no more than two counted interviews from
one organisation. At least four interviewees from four organisations must have
budget or adoption authority; at least four different interviewees from four
organisations must have hands-on responsibility for the target workflow. One
person counts toward only one quota. Record recruitment source, prior ZIAAP
relationship and refusals as well as role, organisation type, workflow
frequency, current tools, baseline time/cost range, failure consequences,
adoption vetoes, procurement path, payment trigger and tested price response.

Run the first six interviews across at least four organisations as the
threshold-setting sample, including at least two authority and two hands-on
participants. Freeze the numerical thresholds and cross-wedge tolerances before
running a six-interview confirmation sample from at least four different
organisations, again including at least two authority and two different
hands-on participants. Threshold-setting interviews may inform the hypotheses
but cannot count as independent confirmation for the buyer, adoption or
portfolio-comparison rows. Confirmation results cannot be used to change the
frozen values.

Interest, praise or willingness to take another meeting is not willingness-to-
pay evidence. Ask for the current budget source, a credible price range and the
conditions under which the person could progress a pilot. Treat every interview
answer as **stated price acceptance**, not revealed willingness to pay. By Day
35, complete the threshold-setting and independent confirmation sequence for
the minimum material improvement, economic hurdle, non-inferiority guardrails
and cross-wedge tolerances specified by the Day 1–10 rule.

### Days 36–70: concierge and comparative benchmark

Run at least three rights-cleared concierge or tabletop sessions with
independent organisations per wedge. Use the same underlying matters in three
baseline conditions:

1. an executed manual workflow using the participant's ordinary permitted
   tools, with actual time and interventions recorded;
2. a frontier general model with its exact configuration recorded; and
3. the structured ZIAAP workflow, with every human/operator intervention
   logged.

For every matter used to support a workflow-structure conclusion, also run the
same-model ablation frozen in Days 1–10. Conditions 2 and 3 may satisfy that
requirement when they use the same immutable model snapshot; otherwise add a
matched structured condition. Repeat the ablation with a second model family
before making any cross-model robustness or operational provider-flexibility
claim. A one-family result may still inform wedge selection, but only as a
model-specific result.

Do not expose the same participant to the same matter in more than one condition.
Use the preregistered parallel or counterbalanced matched-matter assignment,
matched practitioner expertise and identical source material, task instructions
and scoring rules. Count operator assistance as time and cost. Blind outcome
scorers to the condition. Run every stochastic model-assisted matter at least
three independent times. A failed run remains in the denominator; missing and
repeat handling follows the frozen protocol. Where time limits differ, report
them rather than normalising them away. Measure:

- material-issue coverage and omitted-material-issue count;
- source/link accuracy and unsupported-proposition rate;
- legal-quality and citation-fidelity non-inferiority against the frozen expert
  reference and comparator thresholds;
- structured-state coverage and the proportion of accepted state assertions
  traceable to the supporting source, party and version;
- total professional time and human review effort, including verification time
  and rework;
- contestability, including preservation and disposition of disagreement,
  objections and uncertainty;
- invalidation integrity for changed evidence, state, approval or model
  identity;
- error containment, including whether a rejected or failed proposal can alter
  authoritative state or contaminate accepted descendants;
- authority-boundary comprehension;
- verified cost per accepted procedural state and per completed matter; and
- user-rated usefulness alongside the observable measures.

For this programme, **verified cost** includes attributable inference,
retrieval, infrastructure, verification, human review, rework and exception
handling. Failed, rejected and repeated attempts remain in the cost numerator
and never enter the accepted-state denominator. Token price alone is not an
economic measure. Legal-quality and citation fidelity are non-inferiority
guardrails: the programme need not show, and must not claim, superior legal
answers.

The following are hard-stop results in the structured ZIAAP condition and
cannot be offset by an average score: a model or adapter directly mutating
authoritative state; an unauthorised transition; unauthorised data use;
settlement-sentinel leakage; progression despite essential-term non-agreement;
representation that software holds legal authority or creates legal effect; an
invented source presented as verified after the required review; acceptance of
a stale approval; silent provider, model, endpoint or version substitution; or
egress from a condition declared and approved as no-egress.

Unauthorised use or disclosure of real or protected information is a governance
incident, not a retriable benchmark failure. Stop the affected research,
preserve the record, execute the incident and notification process, remediate,
and obtain new accountable and independent approval before any fresh work. The
incident remains in the evidence bundle and the wedge remains Hold unless that
review expressly permits a new study.

A synthetic functional hard stop may be tested again only after a new design
version is recorded and the full affected regression plus at least two sealed,
independently scored holdout matters tagged for the failed invariant are run
under the custody rule above. The original failure remains reported. An
unresolved or repeated functional hard stop is a Fail.

This sample is exploratory, not statistically representative. Report all
results, including failures and operator effort. Do not publish a superiority
claim from this programme without a separately reviewed evaluation design.

### Days 71–90: economics, pilot intent and decision

- Convert observed workflow frequency and effort into a documented baseline
  range rather than a single invented number.
- Test pricing and packaging with at least five qualified buyer-side
  participants per wedge and record stated price response, procurement and
  sales-cycle constraints.
- Estimate implementation, review, support, provider, security and institution
  integration costs; show assumptions and sensitivity ranges.
- Complete an intended-deployment data-flow and authority map for each wedge.
  It must name controller/processor roles, data-supplier authority, required party,
  tribunal or institution permissions, confidentiality and privilege route,
  transfers, training use, retention/deletion design and the authorised adoption
  role confirming that the path is operationally available. Synthetic research
  data cannot satisfy this deployment-feasibility requirement. A named
  competent privacy, legal and—where applicable—arbitration-procedure reviewer
  must assess the map for the target jurisdictions and record the supporting
  basis, limits and unresolved points. This is feasibility research, not a legal
  opinion, compliance approval or authority to deploy.
- Seek written, conditional pilot intent from at least two independent
  organisations for any wedge proposed for selection. The note must identify
  buyer role, scoped workflow, price or budget condition, prerequisites and
  data boundary; a non-binding expression of interest is not a sale.
- For any wedge proposed for Go or Narrow, obtain at least one revealed,
  authorised price commitment to the **exact scoped delivery hypothesis**: a
  deposit, purchase order, signed priced pilot or contract-equivalent budget
  commitment conditional only on enumerated prototype prerequisites. Record the
  offer, delivery model, service labour, price and conditions. A paid manual
  concierge engagement supports only that service offer unless the selected
  go-to-market offer intentionally includes the same labour/software mix; it
  does not establish willingness to pay for a different embedded product or
  workspace. If only interview answers or non-binding intent exist, label price
  evidence Inconclusive and do not call it willingness to pay.
- Complete the wedge scorecards and issue a go, refine, narrow or hold
  recommendation for review. Do not change the roadmap in the same act.

## Challenge-case matrix

These are required benchmark and tabletop cases, not claims that the current
demonstrator already implements them.

| Challenge | Required invariant or observation |
|---|---|
| Deliberately preserved ambiguity | The open point, party positions, reason and treatment remain visible; the system never relabels it resolved |
| Essential-term non-agreement | Gate readiness is blocked and the missing essential term is explicit |
| Individual expectations | Each expectation remains attributable and versioned; generated wording does not overwrite it |
| Non-binding scenario response | ZIAAP does not treat or route the response as an amendment, consent or merits admission; external treatment remains subject to applicable law and adopted procedure |
| Formal incorporation | The legally effective external act—not a software label—governs any amendment; ZIAAP opens a new simulated governing version only after the required evidence is recorded, and prior approvals and descendants become stale |
| Provider/model/version replacement | Prior evaluation and approval do not transfer silently; replacement is re-evaluated and rollback is possible only to an exact approved snapshot |
| Stale approvals | Every behaviour-affecting field change invalidates the affected confirmation, manifest and downstream artifacts |
| Settlement leakage | Unique settlement sentinels remain absent from merits prompts, outputs, logs, dossiers and black-box exports except for expressly permitted typed status |
| Omitted evidence | The strongest omitted item is surfaced or the coverage failure is measurable and reviewable |
| Party objection | Either party can object symmetrically; issue, source, requested response and disposition remain traceable |
| Human pre-assessment | It is recorded before advisory output is revealed, remains private from model context and is evidenced without exporting deliberation |

## Wedge-selection evidence gate

Each cell begins as **Pending** and must end as **Pass**, **Fail** or
**Inconclusive**, with a linked evidence record. Pending means not evaluated and
permits no decision. Pass means the frozen threshold was met; Fail means it was
not; Inconclusive means the preregistered evidence was completed but cannot
resolve the threshold. Neither Pending nor Inconclusive can support Go or
Narrow.

| Evidence | Wedge A | Wedge B | Minimum acceptable record |
|---|---|---|---|
| One buyer | Pending | Pending | Named role and segment confirmed across the independent sample, including budget/adoption authority |
| One painful workflow | Pending | Pending | Frequency, current method, consequence and executed baseline time/cost range meet the frozen problem threshold |
| Adoption authority | Pending | Pending | Decision maker, required consents, vetoes and procurement/procedural path are confirmed by an authorised participant |
| Price and revealed willingness to pay | Pending | Pending | Stated price tests plus at least one authorised commitment to the exact scoped delivery of the kind defined above for Go or Narrow |
| Comparative advantage | Pending | Pending | Meets the frozen minimum material improvement against both executed manual and general-model conditions, satisfies legal-quality and citation-fidelity non-inferiority guardrails, includes the same-model ablation for any process-advantage conclusion, includes uncertainty/repeats and has no unresolved hard stop; two model families are additionally required before any cross-model robustness or operational provider-flexibility claim |
| Implementation burden | Pending | Pending | Integration, human operation, security, privacy, legal/procedural and support work meet the frozen burden ceiling |

### Decision meanings

| Decision | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Go | Every mandatory row is Pass for the original wedge scope and no hard-stop result remains |
| Narrow | A preregistered sub-segment, workflow and feature set independently has every row at Pass and no hard-stop result remains; a post-hoc sub-scope requires a new confirmatory holdout or bounded cycle before it can Pass |
| Refine | Both data rows are Pass, no other row is Fail and no hard stop is unresolved, but at least one other completed row is Inconclusive; approve one bounded, owned and dated retest only |
| Hold | Any row is Fail, either data row is Inconclusive, a hard stop is unresolved or repeated, or the bounded retest remains Inconclusive |

Apply the per-wedge decision first. Then record a separate portfolio outcome:

| Portfolio outcome | Rule |
|---|---|
| Select Wedge A or B | Only one wedge is Go/Narrow eligible, or both are eligible and one is within the frozen “no worse” tolerance on every comparison dimension and beyond the frozen “materially better” tolerance on at least one |
| Both viable — selection unresolved | Both are Go/Narrow eligible but neither dominates under the frozen tolerances or the preferred wedge changes across a credible sensitivity range |
| Neither viable | Both wedges are Hold |
| More evidence required | Neither wedge is selectable and at least one has a valid Refine decision |

The comparison dimensions are strength of revealed buyer commitment,
improvement relative to the frozen threshold, adoption steps and sales-cycle
range, economics/capital burden, and residual legal/data risk. Test every
uncertain assumption across its recorded sensitivity range. “Both viable” is
not permission to choose by preference: run a targeted comparison or record no
selection. Do not default to the original roadmap or the memorandum.

A decision record must name its owner, date, evidence bundle, dissent,
conditions, excluded scope and the separate authority needed for any prototype
work.

## Live consequential pilot prerequisites

The ninety-day programme cannot use a live consequential arbitration. Any later

- end-to-end acceptance evidence for the exact frozen product/configuration and
  use case, including every hard-stop and challenge path;

  accountable operational support;
- tested monitoring, manual fallback, continuity, recovery and stop procedures;
  and
- an authorised prototype/pilot charter with stop conditions.

No repository checkbox, tabletop result or expression of interest satisfies
these prerequisites. Maintain them in a prerequisite register with the exact
use case, required artifact, accountable owner, approver, independent reviewer
where needed, Pass/Fail status, review date, expiry or re-review trigger and
dependencies. The exact disclosure, consent, objection and challenge treatment
for the parties, tribunal and institution must be recorded under the applicable
law and adopted procedure. Every prerequisite must be current and Pass; a
Pending, expired or conditionally failed row blocks the pilot.
