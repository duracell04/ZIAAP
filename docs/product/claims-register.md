## Required qualifications

- **Partner ownership:** always “future,” “intended” or “target design”; never an
  implemented corporate fact.
- **First:** always “aspirational category claim” with competitive
  substantiation pending.
- **Economic outcomes:** always “aims,” “seeks,” “designed to” or “hypothesis”;
  never achieved or quantified without approved evidence.
- **CHF 50,000–500,000:** always an initial target-market hypothesis.
- **AI Act:** classification depends on intended use and the applicable Article
  6 analysis; do not state that every arbitration tool is automatically
  high-risk.
- **New York Convention:** contracting-state participation does not establish
  enforceability of any ZIAAP process or output.
- **JAMS and ICC:** organisational or procedural precedents, not proof of
  ZIAAP’s model or scalability.
- **WorldCC:** commercial-problem context, not value recoverable by ZIAAP.
- **Public feedback:** “submitted” describes the browser workflow only and does
  not prove mailbox delivery; FormSubmit is temporary and must not receive
  confidential, privileged, case-specific or sensitive information.

## Cross-claim prohibited variants

- Working arbitration product or production arbitration service
- AI arbitrator, AI judge, autonomous arbitrator or software holding arbitral office
- Validated, secure, binding, signed, appointed, enforceable or award without a precise future or negative qualification and supporting evidence
- Prediction of a future dispute result
- Proof of identity, consent, provider execution, runtime or legal effect
- Demonstrated comprehension, cost reduction, speed improvement, capacity, scalability or workload reduction before real evidence is recorded
- Unqualified “first,” “faster,” “predictable,” “economically viable” or “scalable”
