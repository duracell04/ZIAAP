# Institutional Brand Book and Legal Interface Design System

**Subtitle:** Brand identity, semantic status grammar, and shadcn/ui implementation specification  
**Status:** Hackathon implementation baseline  
**Version:** 1.0

---

## 1. Purpose

This document governs both:

1. the external institutional identity of the product; and
2. the visual and linguistic representation of facts, assertions, evidence, AI analysis, uncertainty, review, and final human authority inside the product.

The brand is not a decorative layer. It is part of the procedural architecture.

The interface must help users understand:

- what a proposition is;
- where it came from;
- what supports it;
- whether it is contested;
- whether it was generated by AI;
- whether a human reviewed it;
- who owns the final decision.

The product uses **shadcn/ui as its implementation layer**. shadcn provides accessible, composable primitives. This design system assigns those primitives legal and procedural meaning.

> **shadcn/ui provides the component foundation. The brand book defines the legal meaning.**

## 2. Brand foundation

### Mission

Create transparent AI-native legal infrastructure that helps parties, lawyers, and arbitrators structure contracts, evidence, arguments, uncertainty, and decisions while preserving procedural voice and human accountability.

### Positioning

The product is an **institutional legal decision environment**, not a chatbot, AI personality, or autonomous judge.

It combines:

- classical legal legitimacy;
- modern computational structure;
- visible reasoning;
- contestability;
- human-owned authority.

### Brand promise

## Legitimacy through inspectability.

### External tagline

## Contract clarity before conflict.

### Product tagline

## Reasoning made visible. Judgment kept accountable.

### Positioning sentence

> Open-source AI infrastructure for contract alignment, dispute prevention, transparent reasoning, and human-owned arbitral decision-making.

### Brand character

The brand should feel:

- calm;
- precise;
- neutral;
- serious;
- technically sophisticated;
- procedurally fair;
- reviewable;
- institutionally credible.

The visual concept is:

> **A classical legal institution translated into a transparent computational protocol.**

## 3. Core design principles

### 3.1 Authority through structure

Authority comes from coherent procedure, sources, review states, and visible responsibility. The interface communicates institutional seriousness without presenting computation as legal authority.

### 3.2 Trust through inspectability

Every material proposition should expose its provenance, support, objections, uncertainty, and review status.

### 3.3 Clarity with preserved complexity

The system simplifies navigation and comparison while preserving legally material nuance.

### 3.4 Evidence before recommendation

The product presents issues, evidence, and competing arguments before revealing a preferred disposition.

### 3.5 Symmetry between parties

Both parties receive equivalent visual treatment, component density, ordering rules, typography, and interaction controls.

### 3.6 Calibrated reliance

AI output appears provisional and reviewable. Human-reviewed and final states appear visibly distinct.

### 3.7 Protective friction

Consequential actions include deliberate review steps. Speed serves comprehension and responsibility rather than premature closure.

### 3.8 Accessibility as procedural fairness

Meaning is communicated through labels, shapes, borders, icons, and text in addition to color.

## 4. Visual identity

### 4.1 Art direction

The visual language combines:

- warm legal-document surfaces;
- classical civic architecture;
- thin institutional rules;
- modular grids;
- evidence and reasoning diagrams;
- restrained line iconography;
- technical provenance details;
- generous whitespace.

The page-one visual identity establishes the correct direction: ivory paper, Juris Ink, restrained Civic Red, classical columns, a formal grid, and a visible I0 â†’ I1 â†’ I2 protocol progression.

### 4.2 Logo

The primary mark combines the letter **Z** with classical columns and horizontal institutional rules.

Approved logo forms:

- full horizontal lockup;
- emblem only;
- single-color Juris Ink;
- Juris Ink with Civic Red accent;
- reversed ivory version on Juris Ink.

Logo rules:

- preserve clear space equal to the width of the central Z stroke;
- use the emblem at a minimum digital size of 24 px;
- use the full lockup at a minimum width of 160 px;
- preserve flat color and crisp lines;
- use the wordmark as a logo asset rather than reproducing it with a UI font.

### 4.3 Imagery

Preferred imagery:

- architectural line drawings;
- archival legal materials;
- restrained institutional photography;
- contracts and evidence shown as structured records;
- diagrams of reasoning, provenance, and procedural flow;
- human review shown through hands, annotations, signatures, or professional workspaces.

The visual system excludes:

- humanoid robots;
- glowing brains;
- neon AI imagery;
- theatrical gavels;
- celebratory legal-outcome graphics;
- aggressive courtroom imagery;
- decorative gradients;
- visual metaphors suggesting that AI itself possesses authority.

### 4.4 Motion

Motion communicates:

- a change in procedural status;
- a newly connected source;
- an updated review state;
- a transition between case stages.

Motion remains restrained, functional, and reversible. Legal outcomes receive no celebratory animation.

| **Warning Amber** | `#B88746` | Ambiguity, missing evidence, review required |
| **Procedural Green** | `#5F7C6A` | Verified, reviewed, accepted, completed |

### 5.2 Accessible semantic foregrounds

The lighter semantic colors function primarily as borders, icons, and background accents. Text uses darker companion colors.

| Meaning | Foreground |
|---|---:|
| AI reasoning text | `#3F5E68` |
| Contested text | `#842820` |
| Warning text | `#6F4A18` |
| Reviewed text | `#3F5F4C` |

### 5.3 Brand ratio

The external identity should approximate:

- 70% Forum Ivory and Document Paper;
- 15% Juris Ink;
- 7% Stone Gray and Archive Sand;
- 5% Transparent Blue-Gray;
- 3% Civic Red.

Civic Red functions like a legal stamp: rare, consequential, and precise.

### 5.4 Functional color rules

- **Juris Ink** carries authority and primary actions.
- **Reasoning Blue** carries AI analysis and active inspection.
- **Civic Red** marks objections, conflict, destructive actions, and material exceptions.
- **Warning Amber** marks incomplete, ambiguous, or review-dependent states.
- **Procedural Green** marks verified and reviewed states.
- **Transparent Blue-Gray** supports AI provenance and technical structure. It is not used for normal-size body text.
- Color always appears with a textual label, icon, border pattern, or other non-color cue.

### 5.5 Theme scope

The hackathon prototype ships in **light mode**, preserving the legal-document character of the system. A dark institutional theme becomes a later accessibility and working-environment variant.

## 7. Layout and spatial system

### Grid

- 4 px base unit;
- 8 px primary spacing rhythm;
- 12-column desktop grid;
- generous outer margins;
- strong alignment between evidence, reasoning, and review panels.

### Radius

Use a restrained radius:

```css
--radius: 0.375rem;
```

Legal records should feel precise and constructed. Soft, highly rounded cards would weaken the institutional character.

### Borders

- standard divider: 1 px Stone Gray;
- active or material card: 1.5 px;
- final human decision: 2 px double or nested Juris Ink border;
- AI extraction: dashed border;
- AI inference: dotted or segmented border;
- contested status: Civic Red left rule plus label.

### Shadows

Shadows remain subtle and rare:

- cards rely primarily on borders;
- dialogs and sheets may use a low-elevation shadow;
- final authority is communicated through structure and labeling rather than dramatic elevation.

### Information order

The default sequence is:

1. issue;
2. source evidence;
3. party positions;
4. AI extraction;
5. AI inference;
6. uncertainty and counterargument;
7. human review;
8. final decision;
9. audit history.

## 8. Semantic legal grammar

A single `status` field is insufficient because one proposition can be AI-generated, contested, partially supported, and human-reviewed at the same time.

Every material proposition therefore uses four semantic dimensions.

### 8.1 Provenance

Who or what produced the content?

- source record;
- claimant;
- respondent;
- counsel;
- AI extraction;
- AI inference;
- human reviewer;
- tribunal.

### 8.2 Epistemic type

What kind of proposition is it?

- established fact;
- party assertion;
- extracted statement;
- inference;
- legal rule;
- interpretation;
- forecast;
- proposed disposition;
- conclusion.

### 8.3 Procedural and evidence status

How does the case record currently treat it?

- agreed;
- contested;
- unanswered;
- partially accepted;
- supported;
- partially supported;
- missing evidence;
- contradictory evidence;
- uncertain.

### 8.4 Authority and review status

What level of authority does it carry?

- AI-generated;
- pending review;
- human-reviewed;
- modified by reviewer;
- rejected by reviewer;
- adopted by arbitrator;
- final human decision.

### 8.5 Critical constitutional rule

## An AI inference must never visually resemble an established fact.

### 8.6 Visual treatments

| Semantic state | Border | Background | Required label | Required metadata |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Established fact | solid Juris Ink | Document Paper | `ESTABLISHED FACT` | supporting source |
| Party assertion | solid Stone Gray with party marker | Archive Sand tint | `CLAIMANT ASSERTION` or `RESPONDENT ASSERTION` | party and submission |
| AI extraction | dashed Blue-Gray | pale blue-gray | `AI EXTRACTION` | source passage and extraction time |
| AI inference | dotted Reasoning Blue | pale blue-gray | `AI INFERENCE` | sources, assumptions, uncertainty |
| Contested modifier | Civic Red left rule | pale red tint | `CONTESTED` | objecting party and reason |
| Missing evidence | dashed Warning Amber | pale amber | `MISSING EVIDENCE` | required evidence |
| Uncertain modifier | amber band or icon | pale amber | `UNCERTAIN` | reason and sensitivity |
| Human-reviewed | green confirmation mark | pale green | `HUMAN REVIEWED` | reviewer, date, outcome |
| Final human decision | double Juris Ink border | neutral institutional surface | `FINAL HUMAN DECISION` | decision-maker, date, version |

A contested marker is a modifier, not a replacement for provenance. An `AI INFERENCE` may also carry `CONTESTED`, `PARTIAL EVIDENCE`, and `HUMAN REVIEWED`.

### 9.5 Component API principle

Semantic dimensions remain separate in the component API.

```tsx
<ReasoningCard
  kind="ai-inference"
  proceduralStatus="contested"
  evidenceStatus="partial"
  reviewStatus="pending"
  title="Delivery occurred after the contractual deadline"
  proposition="The available record suggests delivery on 18 March."
  sources={[
    { id: "EV-014", label: "Delivery receipt, p. 2" }
  ]}
  uncertainty="The signed receipt contains an unclear timestamp."
  counterfactual="The conclusion changes if the carrier log confirms 13 March."
/>
```

### 9.6 Required fields for material cards

Every material legal card contains:

- proposition;
- source or source status;

- procedural status;
- uncertainty where relevant;
- review state;
- timestamp;
- version;
- available correction or challenge action.

Preferred labels:

- `Review reasoning`
- `Show source`
- `Add evidence`
- `Correct record`
- `Contest inference`

Use Archive Sand or outline treatment:

- `Compare positions`
- `View contrary evidence`

Civic Red is reserved for:

- `Mark as contested`
- `Reject conclusion`

- AI verdict
- objective truth
- unbiased decision
- guaranteed outcome
- case solved
- instant justice
- AI decides

### Primary psychological objective

> I can inspect the record, compare both sides, resist automation bias, and retain decision control.

### Characteristics

- symmetrical party treatment;
- source provenance;
- competing interpretations;
- uncertainty and sensitivity analysis;
- independent assessment before AI recommendation;

## 15. Accessibility requirements

The implementation targets **WCAG 2.2 AA**.

Required rules:

- normal-size text reaches a 4.5:1 contrast ratio;
- large text reaches a 3:1 contrast ratio;
- focus rings remain visible;
- every interactive element supports keyboard operation;
- color is reinforced by label, shape, pattern, border, or icon;
- icons include accessible names where meaning-bearing;
- dialogs, sheets, tabs, accordions, and tooltips use accessible shadcn/Radix behavior;
- status changes are announced to assistive technologies;
- reading order matches visual order;
- tables provide headers and accessible captions;
- animations respect reduced-motion preferences.

## 18. Acceptance criteria

The interface is ready for the hackathon when:

- every material proposition displays its type and provenance;
- AI extraction and AI inference are visibly different from established facts;
- contested status remains visible wherever the proposition appears;
- evidence can be opened from the proposition;
- missing evidence is explicit;
- uncertainty includes a reason or sensitivity condition;
- human review shows reviewer, time, and outcome;
- final human authority is unmistakable;
- party treatment is visually symmetrical;
- recommendation follows evidence and issue structure;
- Civic Red remains reserved for consequential states;
- color is never the only semantic cue;
- the primary workflow works by keyboard;
- the case ledger records meaningful state changes;
- the product language distinguishes analysis, recommendation, approval, and decision.
