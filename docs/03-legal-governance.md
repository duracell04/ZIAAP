# Legal Governance

## AI-Native Procedure, Human Legal Authority, Simulation-Only Showcase

## 1. Governing rule

ZIAAP may structure information, compare positions, apply confirmed deterministic
rules, expose uncertainty, and generate provisional artifacts. Software does not
hold arbitral office. Any future legally consequential decision remains the
responsibility of a properly appointed human under applicable law and procedure.

The present showcase is synthetic, `simulation_only`, and has
`legalEffect: false`. It performs no legal or institutional appointment, identity
verification, production signature, award, enforcement, or independent
validation.

## 2. Authority hierarchy

1. mandatory law and public policy;
2. valid party agreement;
3. applicable arbitration law and rules;
4. valid future appointment and procedural decisions of the human arbitrator;
5. the acknowledged protocol configuration; and
6. advisory or mechanical ZIAAP outputs.

The prototype demonstrates only the lower governance layers and simulated
records. It does not establish items 1–4 for a real matter.

## 3. Consent separation

The interface keeps separate:

- party-profile confirmation;
- exact contractual-version confirmation;
- acknowledgement of an eligible simulation artifact;
- acknowledgement of the exact manifest hash;
- simulated disclosure review and arbitrator acceptance;
- optional simulated settlement consent; and
- simulated human review.

No checkbox proves identity, authority, informed consent, provenance, legal form,
or signature.

## 4. Execution authority

Illustrative fixtures are `illustrative_only`. Successful live model calls are
`executed_unverified`. Neither is validated or authoritative. `failed` blocks
acknowledgement and ceremony. `validated` is reserved for future independent
evaluation and cannot be created by the showcase or by the model under test.

The model may report observed safeguards and limitations. It may not give itself
an authoritative pass, approve appointment, or enter an authoritative route.

## 5. Exact protocol manifest

The manifest identifies selected contractual decisions, legal architecture,
Constitution, declared model/prompt/retrieval/tool/engine versions, selected
stress artifacts, fictional human record, and change policy. Its SHA-256 digest
supports internal change detection.

It is not complete build or dependency attestation, provider execution proof,
environment or runtime attestation, a production signature, or proof that a
particular actor consented.

## 6. Human independence and review

The future product concept preserves a human arbitrator’s ability and duty to
control procedure, address challenges, evaluate sources and objections, require
more evidence, and adopt, modify, or reject protocol output. The showcase uses a
fictional person and a non-operative simulated record only.

## 7. Equality and contestability

Material propositions identify source and actor. Party assertions, source text,
AI-supported analysis, legal constraints, deterministic calculations, proposed
options, and agreed text remain visually distinct. Missing evidence and competing
positions are preserved. Party-label symmetry is an illustrative stress case,
not proof of universal fairness.

## 8. Mechanical and legal outputs

Confirmed arithmetic may be reproduced by deterministic code. Legal
interpretation, enforceability, credibility, causation, mandatory-law effects,
and remedies remain bounded and subject to qualified review and future human
judgment. The synthetic service-credit result does not decide broader liability.

## 9. Settlement firewall

Settlement is optional and separately activated. Proposal terms, concessions,
and responses are sealed from merits reasoning. A provisional determination may
receive only the shared merits record and permitted settlement-status facts.

## 10. Safe failure

The system stops on missing configuration, credentials, model mismatch, timeout,
provider error, invalid output, stale hash, inconsistent versions, missing
references, or incomplete acknowledgement. A live failure never becomes fixture
success. Rejected transitions preserve prior state and route to deliberate
recovery or human escalation.

## 11. Prohibited claims

Do not claim that the showcase:

- is an arbitral institution or appointment service;
- verifies parties or consent;
- validates a model or legal outcome;
- provides legal advice;
- signs or issues an award;
- processes confidential production disputes;
- attests a runtime or provider execution; or
- creates enforceable rights, duties, or dispositions.

## 12. Final governance statement

> ZIAAP may assist with an explicitly acknowledged process and provisional
> reasoning artifact; legal authority remains external to this simulation and,
> in any future real arbitration, with duly constituted human and institutional
> actors under applicable law.
