# Legal Governance

## AI-Native Procedure, Human Arbitral Authority

**Status:** Normative governance specification

**Principal framework:** Swiss-seated international arbitration

**Legal-status date:** 12 July 2026

This specification is product governance, not legal advice on a particular
contract, appointment, dispute, settlement, award, or enforcement strategy.
Governing law, seat, incorporated rules, representative authority, institutional
acceptance, and current law require qualified review before production use.

## 1. Constitutional Rule

> **An AI-native, ZIAAP-powered arbitrator is a human arbitrator legally
> appointed by the parties who conducts proceedings through a jointly
> configured, tested, and version-locked ZIAAP decision protocol.**

The human and protocol are never collapsed into one legal actor:

| Element | Role |
| --- | --- |
| Human arbitrator | Holds office, remains independent and impartial, controls procedure, exercises independent judgment, renders and signs the award |
| ZIAAP decision protocol | Runs the agreed agent-first process, structures the record, calculates, tests evidence, retrieves within scope, reasons, and proposes a determination |
| Parties | Create contractual and procedural authority through informed, separate, exact-version agreement, subject to mandatory law |
| Platform | Implements identity, versioning, provenance, segregation, safe failure, and audit controls; it does not create arbitral authority by itself |

Software may not accept arbitral office, make required personal disclosures,
exercise legal responsibility, or sign an award. A protocol output is not an
award merely because parties configured it in advance.

## 2. Sources of Authority

Every consequential action identifies its source:

1. mandatory law and public policy;
2. the arbitration agreement and substantive contract;
3. incorporated institutional or ad hoc rules;
4. the valid appointment and procedural decisions of the human arbitrator;
5. the frozen ZIAAP constitution and manifest within that mandate; and
6. product safeguards implementing the preceding sources.

The protocol cannot expand the human tribunal’s jurisdiction, displace mandatory
law, waive a party’s right to be heard, or create authority against a
non-consenting person.

## 3. Authority Matrix

| Activity | ZIAAP protocol | Parties | Human arbitrator | Result |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Contract comparison | Identify divergence and draft options | Select and confirm terms | No required role | Agreed or unresolved contract state |
| Constitution design | Expose choices and version changes | Jointly configure and approve | Review compatibility with mandate | Candidate protocol |
| Calibration | Run scenarios and record safeguards | Approve observed behavior separately | Inspect adequacy before acceptance | Calibration Report |
| Freeze | Canonicalise and hash | Approve identical hash | Accept exact package | Appointment Record |
| Case organisation | Extract and source-link | Correct, object, and submit | Control use where consequential | Contestable case state |
| Mechanical calculation | Apply accepted formula | Confirm or dispute inputs | Decide disputed premises | Calculation, not automatic entitlement |
| Settlement proposal | Generate in sealed track | Separately consent and respond | No access by default; may facilitate only under an agreed lawful process | Non-binding proposal |
| Proposed determination | Reason under the frozen protocol | Receive notice and challenge | Independently assess, modify, reject, or adopt | Provisional output only |
| Award | No issuance or signature role | Receive and exercise available rights | Determine reasons, operative result, and sign | Human arbitral act |

## 4. Party Consent and Authority

Record as distinct acts:

- platform and data-processing assent;
- confirmation of a party profile;
- agreement to contract language;
- approval of constitution fields;
- approval of each calibration result;
- approval of the exact appointment hash;
- representative authority to conclude the arbitration agreement;
- consent to Settlement Facilitation;
- settlement acceptance; and
- signature or other legally required execution.

Silence, continued use, profile completion, or approval of one layer cannot be
treated as approval of another. The prototype records simulated confirmations;
it does not prove representative authority or create a production signature.

## 5. Human Appointment, Independence, and Review

Before appointment, the human arbitrator must disclose circumstances that may
raise justifiable doubts about independence or impartiality, and the parties must
have the opportunity required by applicable rules to respond. The duty continues
throughout the proceeding.

The human arbitrator must not rubber-stamp the protocol. The product requires:

1. a preliminary human issue assessment before revealing the proposal;
2. access to sources, evidence, submissions, objections, and uncertainty;
3. review of whether the frozen calibration covers the problem;
4. an explicit adopted, modified, or rejected status;
5. independent reasons; and
6. human signature.

The arbitrator remains responsible for jurisdiction, due process, evidence,
credibility, disputed facts, applicable law, contract meaning, causation,
liability, damages, remedies, costs, reasons, and the operative award.

## 6. Constitution, Calibration, and Freeze

The constitution must identify:

- legal architecture and appointed human;
- interpretive, evidentiary, fairness, and commercial principles;
- remedy, discretion, and escalation boundaries;
- settlement activation and firewall policy;
- model, provider, prompt, retrieval, tool, and engine versions; and
- amendment and replacement policy.

Calibration is evidence of tested behavior, not a waiver of later procedural
rights and not proof that every future dispute was foreseeable. Each scenario
records inputs, evidence, required safeguards, result, and separate approval.

The frozen SHA-256 manifest includes every behavior-affecting field and the
approved calibration record. A material change creates a new constitution;
clears prior results, approvals, and appointment; and requires recalibration and
fresh consent. Model availability never authorises silent substitution.

## 7. Equality, Notice, and Contestability

Each party must have an intelligible opportunity to:

- see the material source and relevant passage;
- correct extraction or factual errors;
- distinguish assertions, agreed facts, disputed facts, missing evidence,
  inference, legal analysis, and calculation;
- challenge classifications, sources, assumptions, formula inputs, and protocol reasoning;
- submit contrary evidence and arguments;
- receive notice of material protocol propositions; and
- request a ruling by the human arbitrator.

Calibration includes a party-label swap test. Passing that test does not replace
case-specific equality and hearing rights.

## 8. Mechanical and Adjudicative Outputs

Mechanical results expose formula, version, inputs, units, sources, exclusions,
rounding, and confirmation state. Disputed inputs block an authoritative result.

A proposed determination must display:

- the appointment hash;
- issues, findings, sources, and counterarguments;
- disputed and missing material;
- uncertainty and escalation flags;
- reasoning summary and proposed disposition; and
- **no independent legal effect**.

It may never be labelled award, verdict, final decision, or binding result.

## 9. Settlement Facilitation Firewall

Alignment occurs before a dispute and is neither mediation nor settlement.
Settlement Facilitation occurs only after a dispute, is optional, and cannot
impose an outcome.

For the prototype:

- both parties must consent separately;
- only the shared merits record may be used;
- there are no private caucuses or mediator persona;
- prompts, proposal terms, concessions, and responses occupy a separate state domain;
- the adjudication prompt receives none of that content;
- the merits ledger records only activation, termination status, or an executed settlement; and
- settlement requires matching affirmative acceptance of the same complete terms.

A future full mediation product requires a separate mediator, separate ZIAAP
agent instance, confidentiality terms, privilege analysis, access controls, and
rules for any later change of role. Facilitation by the arbitrator requires the
agreement and safeguards required by the applicable rules.

## 10. Safe Failure and Human Escalation

Block or escalate for:

- manifest mismatch, missing appointment, or unavailable declared identity;
- disputed material fact, evidence integrity, credibility, or missing decisive evidence;
- jurisdiction, applicable law, limitation, or mandatory-law uncertainty;
- serious objection, unequal access, or inability to respond;
- causation, gross negligence, liability, damages, remedies, or costs;
- settlement leakage, privilege, confidentiality, or data-protection risk;
- missing representative authority or execution proof; and
- any attempt to turn a protocol proposal into an award without independent human adoption.

Safe failure preserves the last valid state, marks the issue, prevents misleading
adoption, and creates a human-review record. Confidence cannot override a hard
gate.

## 11. Audit, Confidentiality, and Responsibility

The operational ledger records source, version, hash, actor, authority, notice,
response, correction, calibration, approval, freeze, appointment, objection,
proposal, human review, and signature. It must not store hidden model reasoning,
private settlement substance, privileged advice, or irrelevant telemetry.

Dispute data is excluded from general model training by default. Production
deployment requires role-based access, encryption, retention, incident response,
processor controls, cross-border-transfer analysis, and domain separation for
merits, settlement, privilege, and deliberation.

Responsibility follows control and adoption:

- provider: system security, version integrity, tested calculations, and accurate claims;
- operator: identity, permissions, segregation, notices, and incident handling;
- parties: submissions, evidence, instructions, consent, and representative authority;
- lawyers: consequential advice and work they adopt; and
- human arbitrator: mandate, fairness, independent reasoning, and award.

## 12. Prohibited Claims

Do not describe ZIAAP as:

- software that itself holds arbitral office;
- an “AI arbitrator” without the canonical human/protocol definition;
- a source of autonomous binding legal advice;
- a process that guarantees correctness, validity, enforceability, or recognition;
- a mechanism that binds by silence or model confidence;
- a system that converts arithmetic automatically into entitlement; or
- a settlement system that shares protected negotiation material with merits reasoning.

Permitted public language is **AI-native arbitrator** or **ZIAAP-powered
arbitrator**, always defined as the human arbitrator operating the frozen ZIAAP
decision protocol.

## 13. Prototype Boundary

The current build is a governance, appointment, and dispute-preview prototype.
It is not an arbitral institution, production appointment service, qualified
signature system, full mediation service, case-management platform, or producer
of a legally operative award.

## 15. Final Governance Rule

> **The protocol may run the process agent-first, but every proposed
> determination remains contestable and provisional until the appointed human
> arbitrator independently adopts reasons and signs the award.**
